     CODEBOOK FOR 1962 SURVEY OF FINANCIAL CHARACTERISTICS OF CONSUMERS
                  AND 1963 CHANGES IN FAMILY FINANCES

                         September 15, 1997

     This document reproduces the content of the original codebook
issued for the public version of the 1962 Survey of Financial
Charactersitics of Consumers (SFCC) and the 1963 Survey of Changes in
Family Finances (SCFF).  The SFCC is sometimes known as the 1963 SFCC
because the data for the survey were collected in 1963 with a
reference year of 1962.  The SCFF is a reinterview with respondents to
the SFCC.  A detailed description of the survey methodology and
results is provided in "Survey of Financial Characteristics of
Consumers" by Dorothy S. Projector and Gertrude S. Weiss (Board of
Governors of the Federal Reserve System, 1966), and "Survey of Changes
in Family Finances," by Dorothy S. Projector (Board of Governors of
the Federal Reserve System, 1968).  Copies of the questionniares are
available separately.

     These surveys are major landmarks in the history of wealth
surveys.  They are the direct ancestors of the series of Surveys of
Consumer Finances that began in 1983.  The questionnaires were
directly focused on collecting the details of family wealth.  The
sample design included a special sample of relatively wealthy
respondents selected from tax files; this design allowed the survey
both to improve the precision of the measurement of highly
concentrated assets, and to deal with differentially higher unit
nonresponse among wealthy households.

     The project was conducted under the leadership of Dorothy
Projector for the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System.
Data for the surveys were collected by the Bureau of the Census.  All 
processing beyond data entry was performed at the Federal Reserve.

     The surveys were among the first federal surveys to have a public
use dataset.  Most of the missing data in this file were imputed.
James Smith at the University of Michigan has provided a copy of an
earlier version of the dataset which had been lost at the Federal
Reserve; although there are more missing data items in this file, it
is not certain that it is an accurate reflection of the true amount of
data that was originally missing.

     Three versions of the datasets are made available with this
codebook.  First, a SAS dataset contains merged data from the SFCC and
the SCFF.  The variable numbers in the codebook below refer to the
numbering system in this dataset.  Variables from the SFCC have a
prefix of "V" and those from the SCFF have a prefix of "X."  A copy of
the SAS program used to create this dataset is provided at the end of
this codebook.  Second, there are separate copies of the original
public use version of the SFCC and the SCFF files in ASCII format.
The coding conventions in these files may be unfamiliar to many users.
To conserve space, negative numbers were entered using an "overpunch":
that is, the person who entered the data on punched cards backspaced
over the leading digit and entered a minus sign over the terminal
digit (J=-1, K=-2, L=-3, M=-4, N=-5, O=-6, P=-7, Q=-8, R=-9, and }=-0).
The SAS program presented for the SAS dataset provides one way of
dealing with such data.  Third, the earlier dataset from James Smith
is provided.  A SAS program to read this dataset is also given at the
end of this codebook.

     To assist users in verifying the proper installation of this
dataset, the output of an unweighted execution of the SAS procedure
PROC MEANS and a similar weighted execution (using V4) are given at
the end of the codebook.
